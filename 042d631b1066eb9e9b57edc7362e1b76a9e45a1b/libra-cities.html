Libra Cities

The project

With the rise of drone usage for deliveries and surveillance, Airmatrix wanted to createa a platform for cities to monitor who is currently using the airspace. This platform would integrate AI in the form of feedback after each flight, recommendations after pattern analysis and an assistant chatbot.

Research

Because this product is not something that's readily available already, doing research about direct competitor was not an option. Therefore, I turned to indirect competitors for my quatitative research. I also turned to Airmatrix's other products that include drones for inspiration.

After this, the Senior Product Design and I discussed the general stucture of the app (i.e. the different pages and general function of each of the latter). We agreed that since there is a well established design system and preexisting prototypes for some features (and the tight deadline we had for this project), we could jump straight into High-Fidelity Designing and Prototyping.

The Design

This app comprises of the following pages:

  • Dashboard

  • Drone Operations

  • AI Assistant

  • Teams

  • Analysis & Reports

I will mostly focus on the Analysis & Reports page, as it is the most developed of the pages and required the most components to be created from scratch.

Dashboard

This page was mostly my Senior's work, namely the map portion. I did however create the Telecommunication tab.

For that page, the goal was to allow users to create an area on the map where they want to generate a predicition. To do so, I created a pop-up window to walk users through the process. I chose to break it down in two steps: first, creating the polygon by drawing vertices on the map, then choosing the paramters they want for their prediction. The polygon drawing portion allows users to move aroudnt the map using the grab tool, drawing vertices, and deleting them. The second portion allows customization. Once a prediction is generated, users can click them again to edit them or delete them.

Drone Operations

For this page, I created a simple list allowing users to look at all drone operations. To ensure users see them, I made the text of unauthorized drone detection red as it contrasts well with the background (and is a common warning color). Normal operations are represented with black text. I added a "status" item in the list. To make them stand-out, on-going operations have a green Ongoing status with a green circle, while complete ones are represented in grey with a check mark.

When a list item is selected, the details page comes up. It displays the trajectory of the drone and some details about the operation on the left and notes of the right. The latter includes notes from staff as well as AI-generated ones detailing all events that occured during the operation.

AI Assistant

This page allows users to interact directly with the AI-assistant. The retactable panel on the left show the query history while the right one shows exmaple commands and a textfield to manually type at the bottom. We decided on green for the example commands to make it clear to user they are clickable (as buttons are green in this design system). This color also allows them to standout from the backgroud by having a higher contrast.

Teams

This page allows users to edit the different AI teams used throughout the app to their liking. Each team has Agents within. Teams can be enabled and disabled as needed. They are categorized using the tags in the scrollable bar at the top.

Analysis & Reports

For this page, we decided to break it down in 5 tabs:

  • Overview

  • Revenue

  • Drone Operations

  • Network Performance

  • Weather Impact

For each tab, users can use a dropdwon menu to choose the specific location they want to view information for.

The Revenue tab features wiegets pertaining to money made from drone operations. I chose to organize information by designining one primary large customizable widget. Users can tweak 3 paramters using the dropdown menus at the top: the source of income, the time period and whether it's net profit or revenue. The generated graph is a bar graph tailored to their specific needs.

The Drone Operations tab allows users to view trends in their recent operations. the Daily Flights and Current Operations widgets can be customized to only pull data from a certain type of operation (e.g. UAM flights). The Monthly Drone Operations per Type widget shows a breakdown of the number of operations, as the title says, per month and type. upon hovering a segment of a bar on the graph, more details can be obtained. I made sure to sue colors that contrasted enough from one another while remaining within the dedicated color palette. The Real Time Map is a heat map showing where opearations are currently concentrated.

The Network Performance tab shows information on network usage. The Average Weekly Users widget allows users to view a line graph of the evolution of users over a chosen period of time for a particular carrier. The paramters can, as usual, be chosen a the top. The Number of Users per Provider widget is self-explanatory; designing it did prove challenging however. Choosing shades of green that contrasted enough, fit within the color palette, and still stood out from one another when overlaping took some trial and error, but I eventually settled on the 3 shades shown in the prototype.

The Weather Impact page shows weather information, as it may impact flights for drone. I decided to have both a Weather Alert and Weather Forecast widget, as I felt they served different purposes. The Weather Alert widget is placed at the top left, as it is the natural reading direction for english readers, meaning it will be seen first. This is important as weather alerts are often critical information that can heavily impact drone flights. The Weather Forecast widget gives a more general overview of the weather for every day of the selected period.The Assistant Reports widget gives a list of reports generated by the AI-Assitant detailing prevention methods in case of extreme weather conditions and summaries of past incidents involving extreme weather.

Finally, the Overview tab, as the title indicates, gives an overview of all the aformentioned information. It includes at least one widget from each category so users can, at a glance, have an understanding of current trends and events.
